# Survey Instrument

This document presents the full survey instrument used in the study, including
participant instructions, eligibility screening, demographic questions,
main task questions, and post-task evaluation measures.

The survey was administered online using Qualtrics. Question order within
the main task block was randomized as described below.

---

## 1. Welcome and Instructions

Participants were informed that they were taking part in a research study
examining how people evaluate answers and explanations generated by
Artificial Intelligence (AI).

Key instructions included:
- The survey takes approximately 20–30 minutes to complete.
- Participants should not use external search engines or AI tools
  (e.g., Google, ChatGPT) during the survey.
- Participants would be asked to evaluate how much they trust an AI-provided
  answer and explanation, and to provide reasons for their trust judgments.
- Responses are anonymous and confidential.

---

## 2. Consent

Participants were presented with an informed consent form describing:
- The purpose of the study (examining how LLM-generated explanations influence
  human trust and decision-making).
- Voluntary participation and the right to withdraw.
- Expected duration (~30 minutes).
- Risks (minimal; possible fatigue from reading explanations).
- Benefits (monetary compensation and insight into AI systems).
- Data privacy and confidentiality.

Participants proceeded only after indicating agreement.

---

## 3. Language Selection

Participants selected their preferred survey language:
- English
- Chinese (中文)

(The remainder of the survey was presented in the selected language.)

---

## 4. Eligibility Screening

Participants answered screening questions prior to the main survey:

- Age range:
  - 18–24
  - 25–34
  - 35–44
  - 45–54
  - 55–64
  - 65 or older
  - Younger than 18

- Current location:
  - United States
  - Outside the United States

Participants who did not meet eligibility requirements were automatically
excluded, and their responses were not recorded.

---

Participants provided demographic information, including:

- Highest level of education:
  - Less than high school
  - High school diploma or GED
  - Some college or associate degree
  - Bachelor’s degree
  - Master’s degree
  - Doctoral or professional degree (e.g., Ph.D., JD, MD)

- Field of study or greatest familiarity:
  - Biological, Medical, and Health Sciences
  - Business and Management
  - Mathematical Problem and Logical Reasoning
  - Physical and Engineering Sciences
  - Politics and History
  - Social Sciences and Law
  - Other (free response)

- Gender:
  - Male
  - Female
  - Other

- Familiarity with Artificial Intelligence:
  - Not familiar at all
  - Slightly familiar
  - Moderately familiar
  - Very familiar
  - Expert-level knowledge

- General trust in Artificial Intelligence:
  - Strongly agree
  - Somewhat agree
  - Neutral
  - Somewhat disagree
  - Strongly disagree

---

## 6. Main Task Instructions

Participants were informed that they would see **40 multiple-choice questions**.
For each question:
- An AI-selected answer would be shown.
- An AI-generated explanation supporting that answer would be shown.
- Participants were asked to evaluate their trust in the AI-generated explanation
  and provide a reason for their trust judgment.

---

## 7. Main Task Questions

Each main task question followed the same structure.

### Question Template

- **Category:** One of several domains (e.g., Biological/Medical Sciences,
  Mathematical Reasoning, Physical and Engineering Sciences, Social Sciences and Law).
- **Question:** A domain-specific multiple-choice question with four options (A–D).
- **AI-provided answer:** A single answer option selected by the AI.
- **AI-generated explanation:** A textual explanation generated by the AI.

#### Example Question

**Category:** Biological, Medical, and Health Sciences  
**Question:** A pulse may be felt on a patient's face where the  
A. facial artery crosses the mandible  
B. facial artery crosses the maxilla  
C. superficial temporal artery crosses the maxilla  
D. superficial temporal artery crosses the orbital rim  

**AI-provided answer:** [Shown to participant]  
**AI-generated explanation:** [Shown to participant]

---

## 8. Trust Evaluation Measure

After each question, participants answered:

> “Please rate how much you trust the AI-generated explanation for this question.”

Responses were collected on a **7-point Likert scale**:
- 1 = Not trust at all  
- 7 = Trust completely

---

## 9. Trust Attribution Question

After rating trust, participants were asked to indicate the primary reason for
their trust or lack of trust in the AI-generated result. Options included:

- I am confident in the answer, regardless of the explanation
- The explanation itself is convincing / not convincing
- I trust / do not trust AI models
- Other (free-response)

---

## 10. Randomization and Presentation Logic

- The order of the 40 main task questions was randomized for each participant.
- Each participant saw exactly one AI-generated explanation per question.
- Explanation content varied across predefined experimental conditions.
- All participants completed the same evaluation and trust measures.

---

## 11. Data Handling and Privacy

- No personally identifiable information was collected.
- Responses were stored and analyzed in aggregate form only.
- Raw participant responses are not publicly released due to ethical and
  institutional review constraints.

---

## 12. Completion

Participants completed the survey after answering all questions and were
provided with compensation upon valid completion.
